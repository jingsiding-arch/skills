# China residential lease review checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing a PRC residential lease from the tenant perspective. Start with the high-risk items first. If the contract city is known, verify whether local rules add filing, standard clause, subletting, or dispute-resolution requirements.

## A. Contract identity and authority

### Must verify
- Landlord identity matches the named contracting party.
- Property address is complete and specific.
- Property certificate or other ownership/right-to-rent proof is available.
- If the signer is an agent, there is written authorization.
- If the property may be co-owned or marital property, co-owner or spouse consent is available where needed.

### Risk signals
- Blank identity fields.
- Vague wording such as “经家庭协商同意出租” without proof of actual authority.
- The contract is signed by someone other than the owner with no authorization documents.

### Recommended tenant ask
- Add a warranty that the landlord has lawful rental authority and will bear liability for title defects, seizure, co-owner objection, or invalid authorization.

## B. Term, rent, payment, deposit

### Must verify
- Lease start and end dates are complete.
- Monthly rent, payment cycle, due date, and payment method are complete.
- Deposit amount is clear.
- Deposit refund timing is clear.

### High-risk wording
- “押金不退” as a blanket rule.
- “租金不退” regardless of cause.
- No time limit for deposit refund.
- Blank or handwritten amount fields left incomplete.

### Recommended tenant ask
- Deposit may only offset actual unpaid fees or actual damage caused by the tenant.
- Normal wear and tear may not be deducted.
- Remaining deposit must be refunded within a short fixed period after move-out and settlement.

## C. Fees and utilities

### Must verify
- Allocation of water, electricity, gas, property fee, internet, parking, sanitation, and any other recurring charges.
- Landlord settles all pre-handover charges.
- Utility meter readings and account balances are recorded at handover.

### Risk signals
- The contract only mentions water, electricity, and gas but is silent on property fee or internet.
- The landlord can later shift unspecified fees to the tenant.

## D. Handover and inventory

### Must verify
- Written handover checklist or annex.
- Furniture, appliances, defects, keys, access cards, and meter readings are documented.
- Photos and videos are preserved for move-in and move-out.

### Risk signals
- “物品清单” left blank.
- No defect record for walls, floors, doors, windows, bathroom, kitchen, or appliances.

### Recommended tenant ask
- Make the inventory, photos, videos, meter records, and key/access-card list part of the contract with equal legal effect.

## E. Repairs and habitability

### Must verify
- Who repairs original fixtures, appliances, utilities, locks, water heaters, AC, and structural issues.
- Response timing for urgent and non-urgent repairs.
- What happens if the landlord delays.

### High-risk wording
- The contract only says facilities should be usable but never says who must repair them.
- The tenant bears all maintenance by default.

### Recommended tenant ask
- Non-tenant-caused failure of the premises or original equipment is the landlord’s responsibility.
- If the landlord delays repair after notice, the tenant may repair first and recover the reasonable cost or offset rent.

## F. Landlord entry and quiet enjoyment

### Must verify
- Landlord entry requires advance notice and tenant consent, except emergencies.

### Risk signals
- Broad inspection or entry rights with no notice requirement.

## G. Early termination and breach

### Must verify
- Tenant early termination rule is not purely punitive.
- Landlord early repossession consequences are clear.
- Tenant has termination rights for title defects, safety risks, refusal to repair, and serious interference.

### High-risk wording
- The tenant loses all deposit for any early move-out regardless of actual loss.
- The landlord may repossess quickly with no reciprocal liability.
- “甲方概不负责” for major landlord-side issues.

### Recommended tenant ask
- The landlord must return unused rent and deposit if the landlord breaches or the property cannot be lawfully or safely used.
- Tenant liability should be tied to actual loss, not automatic forfeiture.

## H. Sublease, sale, renewal

### Must verify
- Whether sublease is prohibited or requires consent.
- Whether sale of the property affects the lease.
- Whether the tenant has priority renewal on equal terms.

### Recommended tenant ask
- Add “买卖不破租赁”.
- Add priority renewal on equal terms.

## I. Dispute resolution and governing law

### Must verify
- References current law, not repealed law.
- Dispute clause is clear and not internally inconsistent.

### Risk signals
- The contract still cites the PRC Contract Law instead of the PRC Civil Code.
- It says both arbitration and litigation without a clear election mechanism.

### Recommended tenant ask
- Update the legal basis to the Civil Code and use a clear court venue clause if no arbitration agreement is intentionally chosen.

## J. Pre-sign checklist for the tenant

- Check ID of the landlord and proof of title.
- If needed, collect spouse/co-owner consent or power of attorney.
- Fill every blank field; do not sign blanks.
- Attach inventory, meter readings, account balances, photos, and videos.
- Keep transfer records showing rent and deposit purpose.
- Save chat records about repairs, fees, move-in condition, and any promised concessions.
